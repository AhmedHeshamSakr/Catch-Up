# Final Whole-Codebase Codex Review

_Branch fix/audit-remediation · 6 subsystems · 47 findings (46 code-verified, 4 hallucinations rejected) · grade B · 2026-06-21_

## Verdict

The branch is in solid shape: the headline audit-remediation claims overwhelmingly held under independent verification. Auth and the three deploy paths (CSRF same-origin guard, key-before-GCP ordering, /feedback gating, SSRF IP-pin + streamed size cap, fail-closed-on-nonlocal-bind) are genuinely in place with no auth bypass on any write/run route; the lazy-app double-checked lock, durable `state_delta` session writes, bounded reflection loop, Firestore composite-index coverage, and the safe org_id/user_id/language removal are all real. The remaining defects are real but bounded — one image-layer secret leak, two correctness gaps in the verdict/eval coverage path, two legacy-migration data bugs, and several injection/hardening items that are mostly acceptable deferrals for the single-instance desktop target. The most important systemic issue is a recurring "trust the partial response" pattern: the faithfulness critic, the eval judge, and both legacy-row migrations all silently treat absent/omitted data as safe. Overall grade: **B** — the security perimeter and architecture are sound and the remediation is honest, held back from an A by the secret-baking Dockerfile gap, the partial-response guardrail hole, and two un-backfilled legacy-migration bugs that "green" tests masked.
